Top 5 Movies of All Time

May 14, 2025

At Cinema Courtroom, our mission is to deliver fair, thought-provoking reviews that cut through the hype and get to the heart of every film. While there are thousands of incredible movies out there, a few stand the test of time, trial after trial. Here's our court-approved verdict on the top 5 greatest movies of all time.

1. The Godfather (1972)

Francis Ford Coppola's mafia epic is more than just a film; it's a cinematic institution. With rich characters, morally grey storytelling, and a haunting score, The Godfather redefined what film could be.

Verdict: Timeless Masterpiece

Why we love it: Every line, glance, and shadow feels deliberate and iconic. Marlon Brando's Don Corleone is a masterclass in restrained power.

2. Parasite (2019)

Bong Joon-ho's genre-bending social satire became a global phenomenon. With themes of class division, morality, and desperation, Parasite is as entertaining as it is unsettling.

Verdict: Modern Genius

Why we love it: It's rare to find a movie that balances humour, horror, and critique so seamlessly.

3. Pulp Fiction (1994)

Tarantino's nonlinear crime story broke the rules and made new ones. Pulp Fiction thrives on dialogue, energy, and unapologetic chaos.

Verdict: Cult Classic, Deservedly So

Why we love it: The pop culture references, the soundtrack, the dancing — it's all oddly poetic.

4. Spirited Away (2001)

Hayao Miyazaki's masterpiece blends fantasy, childhood wonder, and spiritual themes into a truly magical experience.

Verdict: Animated Brilliance

Why we love it: It's beautiful, imaginative, and emotionally rich — proving animation is not just for kids.

5. Casablanca (1942)

A wartime love story that's never lost its charm. Casablanca continues to win hearts with its timeless dialogue and bittersweet ending.

Verdict: Forever Romantic

Why we love it: Humphrey Bogart and Ingrid Bergman's chemistry, combined with a script that gave us “Here's looking at you, kid,” keeps this classic forever relevant.
